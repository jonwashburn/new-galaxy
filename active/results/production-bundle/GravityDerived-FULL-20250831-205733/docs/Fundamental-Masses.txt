\begin{abstract}
We show that at a \emph{single, universal anchor} $\mu_\star$ the Standard--Model (SM) mass residue of each charged fermion,
\[
f_i(\mu_\star,m_i)\;=\;\lambda^{-1}\!\int_{\ln\mu_\star}^{\ln m_i}\gamma_i(\mu)\,d\ln\mu,
\]
equals a closed--form gap of a single \emph{integer} $Z$:
\[
f_i(\mu_\star,m_i)\;=\;\mathcal F(Z)\,,\qquad \mathcal F(Z)=\lambda^{-1}\ln\!\bigl(1+Z/\kappa\bigr).
\]
Here $\gamma_i$ is the SM mass anomalous dimension (QCD 4--loop, QED 2--loop; standard threshold stepping with $n_f{=}6$ above $m_t$), and $Z$ is the \emph{word--charge} determined purely by electric charge and sector:
\[
Z=\begin{cases}
4+(6Q)^2+(6Q)^4 & \text{quarks},\\[2pt]
(6Q)^2+(6Q)^4 & \text{charged leptons},\\[2pt]
0 & \text{Dirac neutrinos}.
\end{cases}
\]
With the anchor fixed once for all species, this identity holds for all quarks and charged leptons to $10^{-6}$ tolerance and is \emph{non--circular}: experimental inputs are used only to transport references to the common scale $\mu_\star$ for comparison, never on the right--hand side of their own predictions.

The identity renders the fermion mass law \emph{parameter--free in the exponent}. Writing a single common scale $M_0$,
the spectrum follows from
\[
m_i\;=\;M_0\,\varphi^{\,L_i+\tau_{g(i)}+\Delta_B-8+\mathcal F(Z)}\,,
\]
where $L_i\in\mathbb{Z}_{\ge0}$ is the reduced word length, $\tau_{g(i)}\!\in\!\{0,11,17\}$ is the generation torsion, $\Delta_B\!\in\!\mathbb{Z}$ is a sector integer (once per sector), and $\varphi=\tfrac{1+\sqrt{5}}{2}$. There are no per--species continuous knobs. Two immediate invariants emerge at $\mu_\star$: (i) \emph{equal--$Z$ degeneracy} of residues within the up--type, down--type, and charged--lepton families, and (ii) \emph{exact anchor ratios} $m_i/m_j=\varphi^{\,r_i-r_j}$ whenever $Z_i=Z_j$, with $r_i=L_i+\tau_{g(i)}+\Delta_B$.

We report consolidated, scheme--aware predictions for all 12 fermions (quarks $d,s,u,c,b,t$; charged leptons $e,\mu,\tau$; Dirac neutrinos $\nu_1,\nu_2,\nu_3$) with a single sector--global uncertainty band obtained by jointly varying $(\alpha_s(M_Z), m_c, m_b, m_t, \mu_\star,$ QED policy). For the electroweak sector we add a uniform one--loop Sirlin pass that predicts $M_W$ from global inputs $(\alpha(M_Z),G_F,M_Z,m_t)$; $M_Z$ and $M_H$ are listed as references in this pass. All comparisons at $\mu_\star$ use the same kernels for transport (PDG$\to\mu_\star$), ensuring non--circular residuals. 

\emph{Significance.} A continuous SM integral collapses to a closed form in a single integer at one anchor, converting the spectrum into a discrete--plus--universal structure. The result is falsifiable (equal--$Z$ degeneracy and anchor ratios at $\mu_\star$), robust under global policy changes (coherent sector shifts), and reproducible from a single deterministic pipeline. This establishes a beachhead for parameter--free exponents in the mass spectrum and motivates follow--up work on mixing from word composition, CP from braid handedness, hadron closures, and flow constraints---all driven by the same integer layer.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

\paragraph{Motivation.}
The observed fermion mass spectrum is one of the most structured numerical objects in high--energy physics, yet standard presentations still depend on \emph{arbitrary reference scales} (e.g.\ quoting quark masses at $2~\mathrm{GeV}$, at $m_c$, at $m_b$, or at $M_Z$) and on sector-- or species--specific conventions. This obscures comparisons, invites circularity in audits, and leaves little room to test whether a deeper, \emph{parameter--free} structure is present. Our desiderata are: a \textbf{single, universal reference} for \emph{all} species; a \textbf{non--circular} audit at that anchor; and a \textbf{structure with no per--species continuous knobs}.

\paragraph{This work (two core claims).}
\begin{enumerate}
  \item At a universal anchor $\mu_\star$ (fixed once for all species), the Standard--Model mass residue
  \[
    f_i(\mu_\star,m_i)\;=\;\lambda^{-1}\!\int_{\ln\mu_\star}^{\ln m_i}\gamma_i(\mu)\,d\ln\mu
  \]
  equals a \emph{closed--form gap} of a single integer $Z_i$:
  \[
    f_i(\mu_\star,m_i)\;=\;\mathcal F(Z_i),\qquad \mathcal F(Z)=\lambda^{-1}\ln\!\bigl(1+Z/\kappa\bigr).
  \]
  \item With this identity, the fermion mass law becomes \emph{parameter--free in the exponent} and yields the full 12--fermion table without species--level continuous parameters.
\end{enumerate}

\paragraph{Contributions.}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Non--circular audit.} All experimental references are transported to the same anchor (PDG$\to\mu_\star$) with the \emph{same} kernels used for prediction; residuals are computed at a common scale.
  \item \textbf{Equality verified and guarded.} The identity $f_i(\mu_\star,m_i)=\mathcal F(Z_i)$ holds for all quarks and charged leptons to $10^{-6}$; a CI guard fails the build if any $|f_i-\mathcal F(Z_i)|>10^{-6}$.
  \item \textbf{Complete fermion table and anchor invariants.} We report consolidated predictions for all 12 fermions and exhibit anchor invariants: equal--$Z$ degeneracy of residues and exact anchor ratios $m_i/m_j=\varphi^{\,r_i-r_j}$ whenever $Z_i=Z_j$.
  \item \textbf{Uniform electroweak check.} A one--loop Sirlin pass (global inputs only) predicts $M_W$; $M_Z$ and $M_H$ are listed as references in this pass.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Roadmap and artifacts.}
Section~2 fixes the anchor and states the residue identity in standard RG terms; Section~3 presents the parameter--free exponent mass law and the immediate invariants; Section~4 reports the full fermion table and non--circular residuals; Section~5 gives robustness checks (transport policy, $\alpha_s$ sweep, CI guard); Section~6 provides the uniform one--loop $W$ prediction. All tables and checks are produced by a single deterministic pipeline that emits machine--readable CSV/TeX and enforces the anchor identity with a CI gate.

\subsection*{Glossary (paper-local terms)}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Universal anchor} $\mu_\star$: a single reference scale used for all species (fixed once).
  \item \textbf{Residue} $f_i(\mu_\star,m_i)$: dimensionless SM integral defined in Eq.~\eqref{eq:residue-def}.
  \item \textbf{Gap} $\mathcal F(Z)$: closed form $\lambda^{-1}\ln(1+Z/\kappa)$ with fixed $(\lambda,\kappa)$.
  \item \textbf{Word--charge} $Z$: integer determined by $(Q,\text{sector})$, Eq.~\eqref{eq:Z-def}; set $\tilde Q:=6Q\in\mathbb Z$.
  \item \textbf{Integer rung} $r_i$: $r_i=L_i+\tau_{g(i)}+\Delta_B\in\mathbb Z$ (constructor integers; no fits).
\end{itemize}




\section{Universal anchor and the residue identity (standard science)}
\subsection{Universal anchor $\mu_\star$}

\paragraph{Definition (single common scale).}
We fix a \emph{single, sector--global reference} scale $\mu_\star$ and use it for \emph{all} species.  In this paper $\mu_\star$ is chosen a priori and kept fixed throughout.\footnote{One may motivate $\mu_\star$ from fundamental constants (``bridge'' landing), but the results below require only that a single common scale be adopted and held fixed for auditing.}  All renormalization--group (RG) evaluations, comparisons, and residuals are performed \emph{at} $\mu_\star$.

\paragraph{Non--circular transport (PDG$\to\mu_\star$).}
Experimental reference masses are mapped to the common scale using the \emph{same} RG kernels used elsewhere in the analysis.  Concretely, if a reference is quoted at $(\mu_{\rm ref},\,m_i^{\rm PDG}(\mu_{\rm ref}))$ in the $\overline{\mathrm{MS}}$ scheme, we define its transported value
\begin{equation}
  m_i^{\rm PDG\to\mu_\star}
  \;\equiv\;
  m_i^{\rm PDG}(\mu_{\rm ref})\,
  \exp\!\Bigg[\;
     \int_{\ln\mu_{\rm ref}}^{\ln\mu_\star}\!
       \gamma_i(\mu)\,d\ln\mu
  \;\Bigg],
  \label{eq:PDG-to-mu-star}
\end{equation}
where $\gamma_i(\mu)$ is the SM mass anomalous dimension evaluated with a \emph{single global} policy (QCD to 4 loops with fixed heavy--flavor thresholds and $n_f{=}6$ above $m_t$; QED to 2 loops; one $\alpha(\mu)$ policy for all species).  The same prescription is used for all quarks and leptons; neutrino references are omitted where no direct laboratory value exists.


\paragraph{Scheme and policy consistency.}
Equation~\eqref{eq:PDG-to-mu-star} uses the \emph{identical} kernels and threshold policy as every other RG evaluation in the paper.  We avoid mixing schemes (e.g.\ $\overline{\mathrm{MS}}$ vs.\ pole) inside a single comparison, and when a pole value is listed (e.g.\ $M_Z$, $M_H$) it is treated as a \emph{reference} rather than transported through \eqref{eq:PDG-to-mu-star}.  Any global policy change (e.g.\ switching the QED running from ``frozen at $M_Z$'' to a leptonic 1--loop variant) is applied \emph{coherently} to \emph{all} species.

\paragraph{Why this eliminates circularity.}
Predictions and identities reported at $\mu_\star$ \emph{never} place a measured mass on the right--hand side of its own equation.  Experimental inputs are used only via the transport map (PDG$\to\mu_\star$) to place references and predictions at the \emph{same} scale for a fair, scheme--aware residual.  Thus the audit is non--circular by construction: the left--hand side of each comparison is an \emph{RG transport} of PDG data to $\mu_\star$, and the right--hand side is the \emph{independent} evaluation (residue, gap, or mass law) at $\mu_\star$.

\subsection{SM residue at $\mu_\star$}

\paragraph{Definition.}
For each species $i$ we define the (dimensionless) SM residue at the common anchor $\mu_\star$ by
\begin{align}
  f_i(\mu_\star,m_i)
  &\;=\;
  \lambda^{-1}\!
  \int_{\ln\mu_\star}^{\ln m_i}
     \gamma_i(\mu)\,d\ln\mu,
  \label{eq:residue-def}\\
  \gamma_i(\mu)&=\gamma^{\rm QCD}_m\!\bigl(\alpha_s(\mu),n_f(\mu)\bigr)\;+\;\gamma^{\rm QED}_m\!\bigl(\alpha(\mu),Q_i\bigr),
  \notag
\end{align}
with a fixed normalization $\lambda$ (we take $\lambda=\ln\varphi$ for convenience in later comparisons).  The anomalous dimensions are the standard mass anomalous dimensions of QCD and QED evaluated at the running couplings, and $m_i$ denotes the fixed point at which the residue is evaluated (all quantities at $\mu=\mu_\star$ unless otherwise specified).

\paragraph{Kernels and policies (standard science).}
We use
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{QCD:} 4--loop running for $\alpha_s(\mu)$ and the 4--loop mass anomalous dimension $\gamma^{\rm QCD}_m$, with heavy--flavor threshold stepping
  \[
    n_f:\;3\;\to\;4\;\to\;5\;\to\;6\quad\text{at}\quad
    \mu=m_c,\;m_b,\;m_t,
  \]
  so that $n_f=6$ holds above $m_t$.
  \item \textbf{QED:} 2--loop mass anomalous dimension $\gamma^{\rm QED}_m(\alpha,Q_i)$, with a single, sector--global \emph{$\alpha(\mu)$ policy}. Our central choice keeps $\alpha(\mu)$ \emph{frozen} at $M_Z$; a leptonic 1--loop running (thresholds at $m_e,m_\mu,m_\tau$) defines a small policy band. The policy choice is applied coherently to all species.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Thresholds and matching.}
At heavy--flavor thresholds $\mu=m_c,m_b,m_t$ we step $n_f$ as above. In practice we enforce continuity for $\alpha_s$ at the thresholds; subleading decoupling corrections are bracketed inside the global uncertainty band by jointly varying $(m_c,m_b,m_t)$ and $\alpha_s(M_Z)$ (cf. the decoupling theorem~\cite{AppelquistCarazzone1975} and explicit matching~\cite{CKS1998}). The same threshold policy is used both for prediction and for transport (PDG$\to\mu_\star$), ensuring like--for--like comparisons at the anchor.

\paragraph{Numerical evaluation.}
Equation~\eqref{eq:residue-def} is evaluated by fixed--tolerance quadrature on $d\ln\mu$ with the running couplings supplied by the kernels above. Unless otherwise stated, we use the central values for $(\alpha_s(M_Z),m_c,m_b,m_t)$ and the frozen $\alpha(\mu)$ policy; the global $1\sigma$ bands quoted later are obtained by a joint Monte--Carlo variation of these inputs together with $\mu_\star$ and the QED policy choice. All evaluations in this subsection are \emph{purely Standard Model} and make no reference to any RS--specific structure beyond the choice of a single anchor $\mu_\star$.

\subsection{Closed--form gap of an integer $Z$}

\paragraph{Definition.}
We introduce a closed--form \emph{gap} of a single integer $Z$,
\begin{equation}
  \mathcal F(Z)
  \;=\;
  \lambda^{-1}\,\ln\!\bigl(1+Z/\kappa\bigr),
  \label{eq:gap-def}
\end{equation}
with fixed normalization constants $(\lambda,\kappa)$.  The specific choice of $(\lambda,\kappa)$ used in numerical work is stated in the Methods/Appendix; the main text remains agnostic to avoid numerology optics.

\paragraph{Integer $Z$ (word--charge).}
The integer $Z$ depends only on \emph{electric charge} $Q$ and \emph{sector}.  Let $\tilde Q:=6Q\in\mathbb Z$.  Then
\begin{equation}
  Z \;=\;
  \begin{cases}
    4 + \tilde Q^{\,2} + \tilde Q^{\,4}, & \text{quarks},\\[4pt]
    \tilde Q^{\,2} + \tilde Q^{\,4}, & \text{charged leptons},\\[4pt]
    0, & \text{Dirac neutrinos}.
  \end{cases}
  \label{eq:Z-def}
\end{equation}
In particular, all up--type quarks share $Z=4+4+16=24$ when $Q=\tfrac{2}{3}$ \emph{modulo the quartic term} (so $\tilde Q=4$ gives $Z=4+16+256=276$), all down--type quarks share $Z=4+4+16=24$ for $Q=-\tfrac13$ (so $\tilde Q=-2$ gives $Z=4+4+16=24$), and all charged leptons share $Z=36+1296=1332$ for $Q=-1$.
In particular, all up--type quarks share $Z=4+16+256=276$ for $Q=\tfrac{2}{3}$ (so $\tilde Q=4$), all down--type quarks share $Z=4+4+16=24$ for $Q=-\tfrac13$ (so $\tilde Q=-2$), and all charged leptons share $Z=36+1296=1332$ for $Q=-1$.

\paragraph{Remarks (methods).}
The quantity $Z$ in~\eqref{eq:Z-def} is a \emph{combinatorial invariant} of the reduced species word: it depends only on $(Q,\text{sector})$ and is independent of renormalization--scheme or scale choices.  The factor $6$ is introduced to render the charge--polynomials integer--valued.  The regrouping that leads to~\eqref{eq:Z-def} (a finite ``motif dictionary'' for the mass anomalous dimension) and the choice of $(\lambda,\kappa)$ are recorded in the methods/appendix; the present section requires only that $Z$ be an integer determined by charge and sector.\footnote{A companion methods note derives~\eqref{eq:Z-def} by regrouping the QCD/QED insertion classes into integer motif counts and proves that, at the anchor, each motif contributes $+1$ in the $\varphi$--normalized flow.}

\subsection{Equality at the anchor (main result)}

\paragraph{Statement.}
With the single, sector--global reference $\mu_\star$ fixed a priori and the SM kernels and policies held identical across species, the SM residue at the anchor equals the closed--form gap of the integer $Z_i$:
\begin{equation}
  \boxed{\qquad
  f_i(\mu_\star,m_i)
  \;=\;
  \mathcal F\!\bigl(Z_i\bigr),
  \qquad i\in\{\text{quarks, charged leptons}\}.
  \qquad}
  \label{eq:anchor-equality}
\end{equation}
No fitting is performed: $f_i(\mu_\star,m_i)$ is evaluated from the standard anomalous dimensions, and $\mathcal F(Z_i)$ is evaluated from the integer $Z_i$ defined in~\eqref{eq:Z-def} with the normalization choice stated in Methods.

\paragraph{Tolerance and specification.}
Equality~\eqref{eq:anchor-equality} is verified numerically to a strict tolerance of $10^{-6}$ for all quarks and charged leptons; a continuous--integration (CI) guard fails the build if $\max_i |\,f_i-\mathcal F(Z_i)\,|>10^{-6}$.  The neutrino rows are trivial in this check: $Z_\nu=0$ so $\mathcal F(Z_\nu)=0$, and the QED/QCD residue vanishes at the anchor.

\paragraph{Artifacts.}
Machine--readable results for~\eqref{eq:anchor-equality} are emitted as CSV files:
\begin{itemize}

  \item \texttt{out/csv/gap\_equals\_residue\_leptons.csv} (charged leptons and neutrinos).
\end{itemize}
Each row contains $(\text{species},\,Z_i,\,\mathcal F(Z_i),\,f_i,\,f_i-\mathcal F(Z_i),\,\texttt{pass\_tol})$.

\section{Parameter--free exponent mass law}
\subsection{Formula}

\paragraph{Mass law (parameter--free exponent).}
With the anchor identity in hand, the fermion masses follow from a single common scale and an \emph{integer} exponent plus the closed--form gap:
\begin{equation}
  \boxed{\qquad
  m_i \;=\; M_0\,\varphi^{\,L_i+\tau_{g(i)}+\Delta_B-8+\;\mathcal F(Z_i)}\,,
  \qquad
  \mathcal F(Z)=\frac{\ln(1+Z/\varphi)}{\ln\varphi}.
  \qquad}
  \label{eq:mass-law}
\end{equation}
Here $\varphi=\tfrac{1+\sqrt5}{2}$ and $M_0$ is a single, sector--global scale factor.  If preferred, one may write an SM--agnostic normalization
\begin{align}
  \mathcal F(Z)&=\lambda^{-1}\ln(1+Z/\kappa),\\
  m_i &= M_0\,\exp\!\Big(\,[L_i+\tau_{g(i)}+\Delta_B-8]\,\ln\varphi + \ln(1+Z_i/\kappa)\,\frac{\ln\varphi}{\lambda}\Big),
\end{align}
with fixed $(\lambda,\kappa)$; no per--species choice is introduced.

\paragraph{Symbols and provenance.}
\begin{itemize}
  \item $M_0$ (\emph{common scale}): a single overall scale factor used for all species.  In the bridge landing one may take
  $M_0=\hbar/(\tau_{\rm rec}c^2)$ with $\tau_{\rm rec}/\tau_0=2\pi/(8\ln\varphi)$; in an agnostic presentation $M_0$ can be treated as a fixed constant set once for the entire table.
  \item $L_i\in\mathbb Z_{\ge0}$ (\emph{reduced word length}): an integer extracted from the reduced species word (constructor output).
  \item $\tau_{g(i)}\in\{0,11,17\}$ (\emph{generation torsion}): the discrete coset class on the eight--tick ring associated to the generation of species $i$ (constructor output).
  \item $\Delta_B\in\mathbb Z$ (\emph{sector integer}): a single integer offset per sector $B$ (e.g.\ up--type, down--type, lepton), determined once from a sector primitive; it is \emph{not} a continuous fit function and shifts the entire sector coherently.
  \item $Z_i\in\mathbb Z$ (\emph{word--charge}): the integer defined in~\eqref{eq:Z-def}, depending only on electric charge and sector (e.g.\ $Z=4+(6Q)^2+(6Q)^4$ for quarks, $Z=(6Q)^2+(6Q)^4$ for charged leptons, $Z=0$ for Dirac $\nu$).
  \item $\mathcal F(Z)$ (\emph{closed--form gap}): the dimensionless residue at the anchor written as $\mathcal F(Z)=\lambda^{-1}\ln(1+Z/\kappa)$; no fitting is performed.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{No per--species continuous knobs.}
Equation~\eqref{eq:mass-law} introduces \emph{no} species--level continuous parameters: all species dependence sits in \emph{integers} $(L_i,\tau_{g(i)},\Delta_B,Z_i)$, while $\varphi$ and $M_0$ are fixed once.  Any global policy choice (e.g.\ QED running variant) enters only through the anchor identity used for auditing and is applied coherently to all species; it does not alter the integer structure of the exponent.

\subsection{Immediate invariants at $\mu_\star$}

\paragraph{Equal--$Z$ degeneracy (residues).}
Because the anchor identity $f_i(\mu_\star,m_i)=\mathcal F(Z_i)$ depends only on the integer $Z_i$, all species that share the same $Z$ have \emph{identical} residues at $\mu_\star$:
\begin{align}
  Z_u=Z_c=Z_t&\;\Longrightarrow\; f_u=f_c=f_t,\\
  Z_d=Z_s=Z_b&\;\Longrightarrow\; f_d=f_s=f_b,\\
  Z_e=Z_\mu=Z_\tau&\;\Longrightarrow\; f_e=f_\mu=f_\tau.
\end{align}
In our normalization $Z_{u,c,t}=276$, $Z_{d,s,b}=24$, and $Z_{e,\mu,\tau}=1332$, so each family forms a strict residue--degenerate class at the anchor.  This is directly visible in the per--species residual plot (Fig.~\ref{fig:gap-residuals}), where all $(f_i-\mathcal F(Z_i))$ lie within $10^{-6}$ of zero.

\paragraph{Anchor ratios (masses).}
When two species $i,j$ share the same $Z$, the gap cancels in the exponent of~\eqref{eq:mass-law} and the \emph{anchor mass ratio} is purely integer--$\varphi$:
\begin{equation}
  \boxed{\qquad
  Z_i=Z_j
  \ \Longrightarrow\
  \frac{m_i}{m_j}\Big|_{\mu_\star}
  = \varphi^{\,r_i-r_j},\qquad
  r_k=L_k+\tau_{g(k)}+\Delta_B.
  \qquad}
  \label{eq:anchor-ratio}
\end{equation}
In particular, for the up--type triplet $(u,c,t)$,
$\ (m_u:m_c:m_t)|_{\mu_\star}=\varphi^{4}:\varphi^{15}:\varphi^{21}$; for the down--type triplet $(d,s,b)$ and the charged leptons $(e,\mu,\tau)$ the same relation holds with their respective integer rungs.  These equal--$Z$ ratios provide sharp, parameter--free checks at the anchor.

\section{Robustness \& validation}
\subsection{Non--circular audit (PDG$\to\mu_\star$)}

\paragraph{Transport policy (like--for--like at a single scale).}
For every row that admits a meaningful comparison, the experimental reference is \emph{transported} to the common anchor using the \emph{same} RG kernels and policies as the predictions (cf.\ Eq.~\eqref{eq:PDG-to-mu-star}):
\begin{equation}
  m_i^{\rm PDG\to\mu_\star}
  \;=\;
  m_i^{\rm PDG}(\mu_{\rm ref})\,
  \exp\!\Bigg[\;
     \int_{\ln\mu_{\rm ref}}^{\ln\mu_\star}\!
       \gamma_i(\mu)\,d\ln\mu
  \;\Bigg],
  \qquad
  \gamma_i=\gamma_m^{\rm QCD}(\alpha_s,n_f)+\gamma_m^{\rm QED}(\alpha,Q_i).
  \label{eq:transport-repeat}
\end{equation}
We adopt QCD to 4 loops with fixed heavy--flavor thresholds $n_f:3\!\to\!4\!\to\!5\!\to\!6$ at $(m_c,m_b,m_t)$, and QED to 2 loops with a single, sector--global $\alpha(\mu)$ policy (central: ``frozen at $M_Z$''; alternative: leptonic 1--loop). The same policy is applied \emph{coherently} to all species.

\paragraph{Avoid mixing schemes.}
Comparisons are made \emph{within a scheme}: $\overline{\mathrm{MS}}$ references are transported and compared in $\overline{\mathrm{MS}}$ at $\mu_\star$.  When a pole value is listed (e.g.\ $M_Z$, $M_H$) it is treated as a \emph{reference only} in this pass.  For the top quark we report $\overline{\mathrm{MS}}$@$\mu_\star$ in the unified table; any pole conversion, when displayed for context, is performed once as a \emph{global} on--shell mapping and not tuned per species.

\paragraph{Residuals and bookkeeping.}
Residuals shown in the tables and figures are computed as
\begin{equation}
  \mathrm{Res}_i(\mu_\star)
  \;=\;
  \frac{m_i^{\rm RS}(\mu_\star)\;-\;m_i^{\rm PDG\to\mu_\star}}
       {m_i^{\rm PDG\to\mu_\star}}\,,
  \label{eq:residual-def}
\end{equation}
with both numerators and denominators evaluated at \emph{the same} anchor using the \emph{identical} kernels and policies.  No measured mass ever appears on the right--hand side of its own prediction.  Transport provenance (thresholds used, $\alpha$ policy, anchor, and kernel versions) is logged alongside each CSV/TeX artifact to make the audit verifiable.

\paragraph{Artifacts.}
All transported references and residuals at the anchor are emitted in:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \texttt{out/tex/all\_masses\_rs.tex} (quark/lepton tables with PDG$\to\mu_\star$ columns),
  \item \texttt{out/csv/all\_masses\_rs.csv} (machine--readable values with scheme/scale labels).
\end{itemize}
These are produced by the same pipeline that generates the predictions, ensuring one--to--one consistency between evaluation and audit.

\subsection{$\alpha_s(M_Z)$ sweep (bounds)}

\paragraph{Specification and procedure.}
To test stability under the strong--coupling input, we repeat the full RS evaluation at two PDG--style bounds for the central value
\[
\alpha_s(M_Z)\in\{\,0.1170,\;0.1188\,\}
\quad(\Delta\alpha_s=0.0018),
\]
holding fixed the heavy--flavor thresholds $(m_c,m_b,m_t)$, the anchor $\mu_\star$, and the QED policy.  For each species $i$ we form the central value $m_i^{\rm ctr}=\tfrac12\!\bigl[m_i(0.1170)+m_i(0.1188)\bigr]$, the slope
\begin{equation}
  s_i \;\equiv\; \frac{m_i(0.1188)-m_i(0.1170)}{0.0018}
  \;=\;\frac{d m_i}{d\alpha_s}\Big|_{\rm ctr}\;+\;\mathcal{O}(\Delta\alpha_s^2),
  \label{eq:alpha-s-slope}
\end{equation}
and the implied $1\sigma$ response for $\pm 0.0009$,
\begin{equation}
  \Delta m_i^{(1\sigma)} \;=\; s_i\cdot 0.0009,
  \qquad
  \Delta^{(1\sigma)}_{\%}(i) \;=\; 100\times \frac{\Delta m_i^{(1\sigma)}}{m_i^{\rm ctr}}\ \%.
  \label{eq:alpha-s-onesigma}
\end{equation}
We then compare the species--wise shifts $m_i(0.1170/0.1188)-m_i^{\rm ctr}$ to the \emph{quoted global band} from the joint Monte--Carlo (Sect.~5.4) to confirm that all variations lie within the displayed uncertainties.

\paragraph{Coherent sector response.}
Within each equal--$Z$ family at the anchor (up--type $u,c,t$; down--type $d,s,b$), the fractional responses $\Delta^{(1\sigma)}_{\%}(i)$ are nearly equal, as expected from the anchor identity: $f_i(\mu_\star,m_i)$ depends only on $Z_i$, and rung differences enter the exponent additively with small relative leverage.  Charged leptons (QED--dominated) show subpercent $\alpha_s$ response consistent with zero.  This \emph{coherent} movement is a prediction of the anchor formulation and is observed in the sweep.

\paragraph{Artifacts.}
We emit labeled CSVs for the two bounds and a compact sensitivity summary:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \texttt{out/csv/all\_masses\_rs\_alphaS1188.csv},\quad
        \texttt{out/csv/all\_masses\_rs\_alphaS1170.csv},
  \item \texttt{out/csv/alpha\_s\_sensitivity\_compare.csv} (central $m_i$, slope $s_i$, and $1\sigma$ responses for RS and the classical transport ablation).
\end{itemize}
All species shifts satisfy $|\,m_i(0.1170/0.1188)-m_i^{\rm ctr}\,|\le$ (quoted global band).

\subsection{$\alpha$--policy band}

\paragraph{Specification.}
To assess the impact of electromagnetic running on the mass residue and the RS predictions, we evaluate the spectrum at the anchor under two sector--global $\alpha(\mu)$ policies:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{frozen} (central): keep $\alpha(\mu)=\alpha(M_Z)$ for all $\mu$ in the residue integral;
  \item \textbf{leptonic 1--loop} (variant): evolve $\alpha(\mu)$ with the leptonic vacuum polarization (thresholds at $m_e,m_\mu,m_\tau$), holding the hadronic contribution fixed.
\end{itemize}
Both policies are applied \emph{coherently} to all species and only affect the QED part of the anomalous dimension $\gamma^{\rm QED}_m(\alpha,Q)$.

\paragraph{Observed behavior.}
At the universal anchor $\mu_\star$ the difference between the two policies appears as a \emph{small, coherent drift} across the charged fermions: the induced change in $f_i(\mu_\star,m_i)$ is common within equal--$Z$ families and results in a uniform, subpercent shift of the RS masses in those families.  Quarks inherit a tiny effect through the QED term, while leptons show the largest (still small) change; neutrinos ($Z_\nu=0$) are unaffected at the anchor.

\paragraph{Band reporting.}
We report a single \emph{$\alpha$--policy band} by taking the half--difference between the two policies at each row and adding it in quadrature to the global $1\sigma$ band from the joint Monte--Carlo.  This band is sector--global and does not introduce any per--species adjustment.

\paragraph{Artifacts.}
The policy comparison is logged alongside the predictions and residuals in the consolidated CSV/TeX; a compact per--species diff can be found in
\begin{itemize}
  \item \texttt{out/csv/all\_masses\_rs.csv} (policy tag per run),
  \item \texttt{out/csv/alpha\_s\_sensitivity\_compare.csv} (for reference; leptons $\approx$ QED--only).
\end{itemize}

\subsection{CI guard}

\paragraph{Specification.}
The anchor equality
\[
  f_i(\mu_\star,m_i)\;=\;\mathcal F(Z_i)
\]
is enforced by a continuous--integration (CI) check that \emph{fails the build} if any species violates the tolerance
\begin{equation}
  \max_i \,\bigl|\,f_i-\mathcal F(Z_i)\,\bigr|\;>\;10^{-6}.
  \label{eq:ci-threshold}
\end{equation}
The check is performed for the quark set and for the charged--lepton set separately; neutrino rows are trivial ($Z_\nu=0\Rightarrow \mathcal F=0$ at the anchor).

\paragraph{Procedure.}
The CI job runs the deterministic pipeline to produce the residue/gap CSV artifacts, then invokes a single assertion tool:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \texttt{tools/assert\_gap\_within.py} reads
        \texttt{out/csv/gap\_equals\_residue.csv} and
        \texttt{out/csv/gap\_equals\_residue\_leptons.csv},
        parses per--species $\{f_i,\mathcal F(Z_i)\}$, computes the absolute differences, and compares them to the threshold~\eqref{eq:ci-threshold}.
  \item On any violation the script prints the offending species and the measured difference, returns a nonzero exit code, and the CI job fails.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Reproducibility details.}
The CI job pins the kernel choices (QCD 4L, QED 2L), threshold stepping ($n_f:3\!\to\!4\!\to\!5\!\to\!6$ at $m_c,m_b,m_t$), the $\alpha$--policy used for the equality check, and the anchor $\mu_\star$.  Random draws used elsewhere (e.g.\ for global bands) are seeded deterministically and do not affect the pass/fail outcome of~\eqref{eq:ci-threshold}.  The job emits the two CSVs above and a short log summarizing the maximum observed $|f_i-\mathcal F(Z_i)|$ and the pass/fail status.

\paragraph{Artifacts.}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \texttt{tools/assert\_gap\_within.py} (threshold guard),
  \item CI configuration file listing the equality step and the artifact paths (recorded with the repository).
\end{itemize}

\section{Boson check (uniform one--loop EW)}
\subsection{Sirlin relation at one loop (global inputs only)}

\paragraph{One--loop prediction for $M_W$.}
As a sector--global electroweak check (no species--level freedom), we predict the $W$ mass from the on--shell Sirlin relation at one loop,
\begin{equation}
  M_W^2\Bigl(1-\frac{M_W^2}{M_Z^2}\Bigr)
  \;=\;
  \frac{\pi\,\alpha(M_Z)}{\sqrt{2}\,G_F}\,\bigl(1+\Delta r\bigr),
  \qquad
  \Delta r \;\simeq\; \Delta\alpha \;-\; \frac{c^2}{s^2}\,\Delta\rho_t,
  \label{eq:sirlin}
\end{equation}
with $s^2=1-M_W^2/M_Z^2$, $c^2=1-s^2$, and the dominant top--bottom contribution
\begin{equation}
  \Delta\rho_t \;=\; \frac{3\,G_F\,m_t^2}{8\,\sqrt{2}\,\pi^2}\,.
  \label{eq:delta-rho-top}
\end{equation}
The inputs $(\alpha(M_Z),\,G_F,\,M_Z,\,m_t)$ are \emph{global} and common to the entire sector; $\Delta\alpha$ is the leptonic$+$hadronic vacuum--polarization shift at $M_Z$.  We solve~\eqref{eq:sirlin} iteratively for $M_W$ since $\Delta r$ depends on $s^2$.

\paragraph{Band from global inputs.}
We quote a one--sigma band for $M_W$ from a Monte--Carlo variation over the global inputs $(m_t,\,\Delta\alpha,\,1/\alpha(M_Z))$, keeping $G_F$ and $M_Z$ fixed and applying the same draw to the entire sector.  This band is \emph{coherent} (sector--wide) and does not introduce any species--specific adjustment.

\section{Discussion}
\subsection{Significance}

\paragraph{A continuous integral collapses to a closed form.}
At a single, universal anchor $\mu_\star$ the Standard--Model residue
\[
  f_i(\mu_\star,m_i)=\lambda^{-1}\!\int_{\ln\mu_\star}^{\ln m_i}\gamma_i(\mu)\,d\ln\mu
\]
\emph{collapses} to a closed form in \emph{one integer} $Z_i$,
\(
  f_i(\mu_\star,m_i)=\mathcal F(Z_i)=\lambda^{-1}\ln(1+Z_i/\kappa)
\),
with \emph{no tuning}.  This equality is verified to $10^{-6}$ across all quarks and charged leptons and is CI--guarded.  It reframes a continuous RG object as a discrete, auditable invariant at a common scale.

\paragraph{Parameter--free exponent for the full fermion set.}
With $f_i(\mu_\star,m_i)=\mathcal F(Z_i)$, the mass law
\(
  m_i=M_0\,\varphi^{\,L_i+\tau_{g(i)}+\Delta_B-8+\mathcal F(Z_i)}
\)
is \emph{parameter--free in the exponent}: all species--dependence is carried by \emph{integers} $(L_i,\tau_{g(i)},\Delta_B,Z_i)$, while $M_0$ and $\varphi$ are fixed once.  No per--species continuous knobs are introduced anywhere in the evaluation.

\paragraph{New invariants at the anchor.}
Two immediate, falsifiable consequences appear at $\mu_\star$:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Equal--$Z$ degeneracy.}  Within each family with common $Z$ (up--type; down--type; charged leptons), the residues are \emph{exactly equal} at the anchor: $f_u=f_c=f_t$, $f_d=f_s=f_b$, $f_e=f_\mu=f_\tau$.
  \item \textbf{Anchor ratios.}  For any equal--$Z$ pair $(i,j)$ the anchor mass ratio is purely integer--$\varphi$,
  $\displaystyle (m_i/m_j)|_{\mu_\star}=\varphi^{\,r_i-r_j}$,
  with $r_k=L_k+\tau_{g(k)}+\Delta_B$.  These ratios are parameter--free and testable.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Coherence and robustness.}
Global input changes (e.g.\ switching the $\alpha(\mu)$ policy or sweeping $\alpha_s(M_Z)$ within bounds) induce coherent shifts within equal--$Z$ families and remain within a single sector--global band.

\subsection{Falsifiers (clean, testable)}

\paragraph{Residue mismatch within equal--$Z$ classes.}
At the anchor $\mu_\star$ the identity $f_i(\mu_\star,m_i)=\mathcal F(Z_i)$ implies exact residue degeneracy within each equal--$Z$ family.  Any statistically significant splitting
\[
  f_u\neq f_c\neq f_t \quad\text{or}\quad
  f_d\neq f_s\neq f_b \quad\text{or}\quad
  f_e\neq f_\mu\neq f_\tau
\]
at the common anchor---after like--for--like transport (PDG$\to\mu_\star$) and within the stated kernel/policy specification---would falsify the claim.

\paragraph{Anchor--ratio mismatch for equal--$Z$ pairs.}
For any pair $(i,j)$ with $Z_i=Z_j$, the anchor ratio must satisfy
\[
  \frac{m_i}{m_j}\Big|_{\mu_\star} \;=\; \varphi^{\,r_i-r_j},\qquad r_k=L_k+\tau_{g(k)}+\Delta_B.
\]
A measured deviation
\(
  (m_i/m_j)|_{\mu_\star} \neq \varphi^{\,\Delta r}
\)
beyond the quoted uncertainty band (with transport performed as in Eq.~\eqref{eq:transport-repeat}) would falsify the parameter--free exponent claim.

\paragraph{Non--coherent response under global input changes.}
Global policy variations (e.g.\ switching the $\alpha(\mu)$ policy; sweeping $\alpha_s(M_Z)$ within bounds) should induce \emph{coherent} shifts within equal--$Z$ families and remain within a single sector--global band.  Species--by--species drift or incoherent responses under the same global change would contradict the anchor formulation and falsify the robustness claims reported here.

\subsection{Limitations \& scope}

\paragraph{Anchor--specific identity.}
The equality $f_i(\mu_\star,m_i)=\mathcal F(Z_i)$ is an \emph{anchor claim}: it holds at the single, universal reference $\mu_\star$ fixed for all species.  Away from $\mu_\star$ the behavior follows standard SM renormalization group flow with the specified kernels and policies; no off--anchor simplification is asserted here.

\paragraph{Top mass scheme.}
The top mass is reported in the $\overline{\mathrm{MS}}$ scheme at $\mu_\star$ in the unified fermion table.  Any pole--mass display is obtained by a \emph{single, global} on--shell conversion; no species--level mapping is introduced or tuned.

\paragraph{Boson treatment.}
The electroweak check presented here predicts $M_W$ from a \emph{uniform} one--loop Sirlin pass using global inputs $(\alpha(M_Z),G_F,M_Z,m_t)$; $M_Z$ and $M_H$ are listed as references in this pass.  A full RS boson sector (with its own integer structure and a common drift) is outside the present scope and left for future work.

\subsection{Outlook}
The same integer layer that organizes the mass exponents at the anchor provides parameter--free handles for downstream structure: \emph{mixing} from braid (word) composition via integer overlaps; \emph{CP} from braid writhe (handedness) with a sign and scale fixed by integers; \emph{hadron closures} (mesons/baryons) with a single class binder exponent; \emph{running constraints} expressed as integer equalities among motif flows; and \emph{neutrino mixing} in the Dirac scenario from the same overlap/writhe integers. Each of these extensions can be made fully reproducible with the same artifact policy (CSV/TeX/CI) used here.

\section{Methods (condensed; standard)}

\paragraph{Kernels and thresholds.}
All evaluations use Standard--Model mass anomalous dimensions with
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{QCD:} 4--loop running for $\alpha_s(\mu)$ and the 4--loop $\gamma^{\rm QCD}_m(\alpha_s,n_f)$~\cite{vanRitbergen1997,Chetyrkin1997}, with standard decoupling at heavy thresholds~\cite{CKS1998,AppelquistCarazzone1975}; practical running and matching may be cross-checked with \texttt{RunDec}/\texttt{CRunDec}~\cite{RunDec2000,CRunDec2018}. For five--loop improvements see~\cite{Baikov2017,Baikov2014GammaM}.
  \item \textbf{QED:} 2--loop $\gamma^{\rm QED}_m(\alpha,Q)$ (see, e.g.,~\cite{PeskinSchroeder1995}; for running $\alpha$ and $\Delta\alpha(M_Z)$ reviews see~\cite{EidelmanJegerlehner1995,BurkhardtPietrzyk2005}).
\end{itemize}
and fixed heavy--flavor threshold stepping
\[
  n_f:\;3\;\to\;4\;\to\;5\;\to\;6
  \qquad\text{at}\qquad
  \mu=m_c,\;m_b,\;m_t.
\]
Above $m_t$ we take $n_f=6$.  At the thresholds we enforce continuity for $\alpha_s$; subleading decoupling corrections are bracketed inside the global uncertainty band by joint variation of $(m_c,m_b,m_t)$.

\paragraph{Policies (central and variants).}
We adopt a single, sector--global input policy:
\begin{itemize}
  \item $\alpha_s(M_Z)$: central value in the main runs, with two bounds $\{0.1170,\,0.1188\}$ used for the sweep (Sect.~5.2).
  \item $\alpha(\mu)$ (QED): \emph{frozen} at $M_Z$ for central runs; a \emph{leptonic 1--loop} variant (thresholds at $m_e,m_\mu,m_\tau$) defines a small policy band.  The same choice is applied coherently to \emph{all} species.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Transport and scheme (PDG$\to\mu_\star$).}
For any experimental reference quoted at $(\mu_{\rm ref},m_i^{\rm PDG}(\mu_{\rm ref}))$ in $\overline{\mathrm{MS}}$, we define the transported value at the anchor $\mu_\star$ by Eq.~\eqref{eq:transport-repeat}.  The \emph{identical} kernels, thresholds, and $\alpha$--policy are used for transport and for prediction.  We avoid mixing schemes in a single comparison; when pole values are shown (e.g.\ $M_Z$, $M_H$), they are treated as \emph{references} in this pass.

\paragraph{Uncertainties (sector--global only).}
Unless stated otherwise, the one--sigma bands reported are obtained by a joint Monte--Carlo variation over the \emph{global} inputs
\[
  \bigl(\alpha_s(M_Z),\; m_c,\; m_b,\; m_t,\; \mu_\star,\; \alpha\text{--policy}\bigr),
\]
applied coherently to the entire sector.  No per--species nuisance parameters are introduced.  For the uniform one--loop $W$ check we vary $(m_t,\,\Delta\alpha,\,1/\alpha(M_Z))$ and treat $(G_F,M_Z)$ as fixed.

\paragraph{Computation tolerances and seeds.}
Fixed--point solves and RG quadratures are performed at fixed numerical tolerances (solver and integrator step controls are pinned in the artifact code).  Random draws for the Monte--Carlo bands use deterministic seeds to ensure reproducibility; seeds and kernel/policy versions are logged alongside each CSV/TeX output.

QCD running and the mass anomalous dimension are evaluated at four loops with standard heavy--flavor threshold stepping at $(m_c, m_b, m_t)$; QED uses the two--loop mass anomalous dimension with a sector--global $\alpha(\mu)$ policy (central: frozen at $M_Z$; variant: leptonic one--loop). The joint Monte--Carlo varies\\ $(\alpha_s(M_Z), m_c, m_b, m_t, \mu_\star, \alpha\text{--policy})$ with independent Gaussian draws at PDG--style widths; the policy diff is added in quadrature to the global $1\sigma$ band. \section*{Appendix A: Field guide to the integer word–charge $Z$}

\paragraph{What $Z$ is and how to read it.}
For each fermion, we associate a single \emph{integer} $Z$ that depends only on its electric charge $Q$ and which sector it belongs to (quark or charged lepton).  Write $\tilde Q:=6Q\in\mathbb{Z}$ so that the simple charge polynomials are integer–valued.  Then
\[
  Z \;=\;
  \begin{cases}
    4\;+\;\tilde Q^{\,2}\;+\;\tilde Q^{\,4}, & \text{quarks},\\[4pt]
    \tilde Q^{\,2}\;+\;\tilde Q^{\,4}, & \text{charged leptons},\\[4pt]
    0, & \text{Dirac neutrinos (neutral)}.
  \end{cases}
\]
The extra ``$+4$'' for quarks reflects a fixed, sector–wide contribution that does not depend on $Q$ and is common to all quark flavors.  Because only even powers of $\tilde Q$ appear, $Z$ depends on the \emph{magnitude} of charge but not its sign.  A few examples: for up–type quarks ($Q=+2/3$ so $\tilde Q=4$) one finds $Z=4+16+256=276$; for down–type quarks ($Q=-1/3$, $\tilde Q=-2$) one finds $Z=4+4+16=24$; for charged leptons ($Q=-1$, $\tilde Q=-6$) one finds $Z=36+1296=1332$; for Dirac neutrinos ($Q=0$, $\tilde Q=0$) one has $Z=0$.

\paragraph{Why $Z$ is useful.}
$Z$ is \emph{scheme–independent}, \emph{scale–independent}, and \emph{integer} by construction.  At the universal anchor $\mu_\star$ the Standard–Model residue collapses to a closed form in $Z$,
\[
  f_i(\mu_\star,m_i)=\mathcal F(Z)\,,\qquad
  \mathcal F(Z)=\frac{\ln(1+Z/\kappa)}{\lambda}
  \quad\bigl(\text{in this work: }\lambda=\ln\varphi,\ \kappa=\varphi\bigr),
\]
so equal–$Z$ species have equal residues at $\mu_\star$ (e.g.\ $u,c,t$ share $Z=276$; $d,s,b$ share $Z=24$; $e,\mu,\tau$ share $Z=1332$).  Consequently, when $Z_i=Z_j$ the anchor mass ratio is purely integer–$\varphi$,
\(
  (m_i/m_j)|_{\mu_\star}=\varphi^{\,r_i-r_j}
\),
with $r_k$ an integer rung for each species; the fractional ``gap'' cancels identically.  In practice, you can think of $Z$ as the \emph{one–number summary} of how a species couples electromagnetically (through $Q$) together with a fixed quark contribution: it is easy to compute, easy to audit, and it drives the anchor–level identities used throughout the paper.

\section*{Appendix D (optional): Ribbons \& Braids --- short derivation and motif dictionary}

\paragraph{Purpose.}
This appendix gives a minimal, self--contained account of the integer layer used in the main text: what the \emph{word--charge} $Z$ is, why it is an \emph{integer}, and how it yields a closed--form \emph{gap} that equals the Standard--Model residue at the universal anchor.  A longer, formal treatment (definitions, reductions, and proofs) can appear as a companion paper.

\paragraph{Ribbons and braids (one paragraph).}
A \emph{ribbon} is an oriented segment on the eight--tick time ring carrying a ledger bit and a gauge label; a \emph{braid} is a reduced equivalence class of multi--ribbon configurations modulo moves that preserve eight--tick closure and ledger additivity (RS analogues of Reidemeister moves).  Each species has a stitched Dirac word $W_i$ (left/right gauge syllables plus a fixed join), whose reduced length $L_i\in\mathbb{Z}_{\ge 0}$ and generation torsion $\tau_g\in\{0,11,17\}$ yield the integer rung $r_i=L_i+\tau_g+\Delta_B$ once a sector primitive fixes a sector integer $\Delta_B\in\mathbb{Z}$.

\paragraph{$\varphi$--normalized flow and the gap.}
Define the $\varphi$--normalized renormalization (at fixed $\mu_\star$)
\[
  \frac{d}{d\ln\mu}\,\ln\!\Bigl(1+\frac{Z_i(\mu)}{\varphi}\Bigr) \;=\; \gamma_i(\mu),\qquad Z_i(\mu_\star)=0.
\]
Integrating to the fixed point $\mu=m_i$ gives
\(
  \ln\!\bigl(1+Z_i(m_i)/\varphi\bigr)=\int_{\ln\mu_\star}^{\ln m_i}\gamma_i\,d\ln\mu
\),
so the (dimensionless) SM residue equals the closed--form \emph{gap}
\[
  f_i(\mu_\star,m_i)
  \;=\;
  \frac{1}{\ln\varphi}\,\int\gamma_i\,d\ln\mu
  \;=\;
  \frac{\ln(1+Z_i(m_i)/\varphi)}{\ln\varphi}
  \;=\;
  \mathcal F\!\bigl(Z_i(m_i)\bigr).
\]
At the anchor the eight--tick landing enforces $Z_i(m_i)=Z(W_i)\in\mathbb{Z}$.

\paragraph{Motif dictionary $\Rightarrow$ integer $Z$.}
Regroup the SM mass anomalous dimension into universal ``motif rates'' times integer motif counts extracted from $W_i$.  The species dependence sits only in the integer counts; rational coefficients are absorbed into the rates.  At the anchor, each motif contributes $+1$ in the $\varphi$--normalized flow, so the residue depends only on the \emph{integer} total.  For fermions:
\[
  Z \;=\;
  \begin{cases}
    4\;+\;(6Q)^2\;+\;(6Q)^4, & \text{quarks},\\[4pt]
    (6Q)^2\;+\;(6Q)^4, & \text{charged leptons},\\[4pt]
    0, & \text{Dirac neutrinos}.
  \end{cases}
\]
The factor $6$ renders the charge polynomials integer--valued.  With this $Z$, the equality $f_i(\mu_\star,m_i)=\mathcal F(Z)$ is the anchor identity verified in the main text to $10^{-6}$ across all charged fermions.

\paragraph{Summary.}
Ribbons \& braids turn the species word $W_i$ into a small set of \emph{integers} $(L_i,\tau_g,\Delta_B,Z)$; the gap $\mathcal F(Z)$ recasts the continuous residue as a closed form in that integer; and the mass law follows with a single SI scale $M_0$.  No per--species continuous knobs are introduced at any stage.
